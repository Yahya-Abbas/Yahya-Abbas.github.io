About

I am a Master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Concordia University (GPA: 4.3/4.3), with research interests in deep learning, large language models, and computer vision. My work has spanned neural network compression, generative adversarial networks, probabilistic forecasting, and most recently, trustworthy AI.

My current research investigates whether LLMs can serve as reliable co-auditors for machine learning fairness — evaluating their ability to recommend appropriate metrics and identify issues across diverse application contexts. This sits at the intersection of LLM evaluation and scalable AI oversight, a challenge that grows more pressing as AI systems become more capable.

Before graduate school, I spent two years as a Data Scientist at Dell Technologies, where I led research on conditional GANs for synthetic 5G traffic generation (U.S. patent pending) and built deep learning forecasting models for multi-city telecom networks. My BSc thesis at the American University in Cairo introduced a multi-knowledge distillation framework for neural network compression, later published in IJCDS.
